What about your wallet ?




Now, what if I was saying that not only Surveillance Capitalism steals your time (which is already a big problem) but also your money? Would you quit your life to live in Siberia? Because that's the truth — let me explain.

A study, “Discrimination grounds and personalised pricing” (Internet Policy Review), surveyed 727 Dutch consumers on 25 types of data (“grounds”) that companies might use for personalized pricing. It found that people generally consider such pricing unfair. The grounds included both legally protected traits (e.g., race, religion) and unregulated traits (e.g., device type, browsing behavior, intelligence).

Different factors make Surveillance Capitalism increase the prices of the market, ultimately making the money you earn less valuable. I have selected two that I found were the most relevant to Surveillance Capitalism:


1. You Pay More Because Companies Know Exactly How Much You’ll Pay

The more data companies collect about you, the more precisely they can personalize prices. Examples:

  • If your browsing history shows you shop in expensive stores, your prices rise.
  • If you urgently search for flights, your flight prices jump.

This is called dynamic pricing or price discrimination, and Surveillance Capitalism makes it brutally accurate.


2. Data monopolies kill competition. Fewer competitors = higher prices

Surveillance Capitalism creates giant companies that dominate markets because they own all the data. Example:

  • Google controls search
  • Meta controls social
  • Amazon controls product search
  • TikTok controls discovery

When small businesses cannot compete with these giants’ massive data power, they die. Less competition results in the monopoly power for the giants, resulting in higher prices, lower quality and less choices, and in term, to price inflation across the entire market.


That's how Surveillance Capitalism makes our lives harder.
