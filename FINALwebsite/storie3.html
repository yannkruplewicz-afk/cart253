Do you really know who you are ? Because they know.




A study called “Private Traits and Attributes Are Predictable from Digital Records of Human Behavior” (PNAS) analyzed Facebook “Likes” from about 58,000 users and compared them with the users’ own demographic and personality data. The results were shocking: algorithms could predict Big Five personality traits — and even sensitive attributes like sexual orientation, political views, substance use, and family background — sometimes more accurately than close friends or family.

And while this is already impressive, it represents only the first layer. Surveillance Capitalism goes much deeper. Below are eight additional layers of what it can know about you:


1. Basic Identifiers

What it knows: your name, age, gender, location, devices.
How: You provide some directly, while the rest (IP address, GPS, device type) is automatically transmitted by your phone and apps.


2. Behavioral Patterns

What it knows: your routines, habits, interests, attention patterns.
How: Every click, pause, scroll, and timestamp is recorded, forming detailed maps of your daily behavior.


3. Psychological Traits

What it knows: introversion, impulsiveness, emotional stability.
How: Algorithms compare your “Likes,” reading patterns, and viewing time with massive datasets to statistically infer personality traits.


4. Sensitive Personal Attributes

What it knows: political beliefs, sexual orientation, religion, substance use.
How: Your digital footprint correlates with known indicators — from the pages you follow to the places you visit — allowing accurate inference without your explicit disclosure.


5. Mental & Physical Health Signals

What it knows: depression risk, anxiety levels, sleep quality, mood cycles.
How: Sensor data, usage rhythms, location stability, and even typing patterns reveal changes that machine-learning models interpret as health markers.


6. Predictive Models of Future Behavior

What it knows: what you'll buy, how you'll vote, when you might move or switch jobs.
How: Systems analyze your past actions and compare them to millions of similar profiles to forecast your next moves with high accuracy.


7. Vulnerability Profiling

What it knows: when you're lonely, tired, stressed, bored, or easily influenced.
How: Late-night usage, rapid checking, slow scrolling, and irregular patterns are interpreted as signs of emotional vulnerability.


8. Behavioral Manipulation

What it knows: what persuades you — and how to influence your decisions.
How: Platforms experiment with micro-adjusted content to shape your emotions, purchases, beliefs, and behavior.
