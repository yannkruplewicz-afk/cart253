Ever heard of Aza Raskin ?




Aza Raskin is an American engineer notably renowned for creating the infinite scroll. Infinite scroll continuously loads content as you move down a page. To do this effectively, platforms need to know you — your habits, preferences, and responses. Every scroll, click, hover, or pause is logged, and time spent on each post is measured. Does that ring a bell ? It's surveillance capitalism.

A study called "Disrupting Social Media Habits", was led in Denmark and showed that participants spent on average 3 hours scrolling per day on weekdays. Similarly, another study (DataReportal 2025), led in the US and other countries, showed that young adults spend on average 3 to 5 hours a day on social media apps.


If we multiply 3 hours/day × 5 billion internet users, then we realize that globally, we’re spending 15 billion hours/day scrolling on devices.

Now, if we divide this 15 billion hours/day by the number of hours in a year (8766), we found that... Each day, 1.71 million years of human time are spent scrolling. In other words, this represents 21,400 lifetimes a day.


So who has the 'control' now ? During the era of the previous 'load more' and 'pagination' scrollings (image 1), the time spent on screens was significantly lower. Infinite scrolling made the difference.

To summarize, surveillance Capitalism, the act of collecting people's data without their consent to make potential profit is massively used in this infinite scrolling. There, we are being exploited in two ways. Our data are being collected and they are being used against us to make the algorithms work better by exploiting us even more, resulting in a massive waste of time for the user who can easily fall in that addiction.
